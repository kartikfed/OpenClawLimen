# Biochar Carbon Credit Market Analysis
*Research compiled March 2, 2026 — for PrithviPoshan investor presentations*

## Executive Summary

Biochar carbon credits represent one of the most promising **durable carbon removal** (CDR) technologies. Unlike traditional carbon offsets (avoided emissions), biochar physically removes CO2 from the atmosphere and locks it away for 100-1000+ years. The market is nascent but growing rapidly, with India positioned as a particularly high-potential region.

**Key Numbers:**
- Global CDR market: **$787 million** spent, **44 million tonnes** sold
- Only **2.9%** of purchases have been delivered (massive delivery gap)
- Puro.earth biochar pricing: **€131.27/tonne** (~$140/tonne)
- India potential: Can sequester **53%** of national GHG emissions via biochar (highest % among major economies)

---

## What Makes Biochar Different

### Carbon Removal vs. Carbon Avoidance

Traditional carbon credits (forestry, renewable energy) represent **avoided emissions** — preventing CO2 that would have been released. These have faced significant scrutiny for:
- Overestimated sequestration claims
- Double-counting
- Questionable "additionality" (would the reduction have happened anyway?)

Biochar is **engineered carbon removal** — physically capturing atmospheric carbon and storing it durably. This distinction matters increasingly to:
- Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)
- Oxford Principles for Net Zero Aligned Offsetting
- Corporate buyers seeking credible net-zero claims

### Durability Advantage

| Method | Storage Duration | Risk |
|--------|-----------------|------|
| Forest offsets | Decades | Fire, disease, logging reversal |
| Soil carbon | Years-decades | Tillage releases, climate changes |
| **Biochar** | **100-1000+ years** | **Minimal — stable carbon structure** |
| Geological (BECCS/DAC) | 1000+ years | Technology risk, high cost |

## Market Structure

### Certification Standards

**1. Puro.earth (Finland)**
- First standard focused exclusively on durable carbon removal
- Issues CORCs (CO2 Removal Certificates)
- €131.27/tonne average price
- Requires 100+ year storage durability
- Biochar is one of 5 certified methods
- ICROA-endorsed

**2. Verra VCS (Verified Carbon Standard)**
- World's largest GHG crediting program
- 1.3B+ tonnes reduced/removed
- 2,579+ projects in 132+ countries
- Issues VCUs (Verified Carbon Units)
- Broader scope (not just removal)

**3. European Biochar Certificate (EBC)**
- Quality standard for biochar production
- Mandatory in Switzerland, voluntary elsewhere
- Focuses on safe production processes
- Published by Ithaka Institute

**4. International Biochar Initiative (IBI)**
- Industry body, not crediting program
- Provides classification tools, resources
- Advocacy and research coordination

### Quality Criteria (Critical for Credibility)

Per OffsetGuide.org, high-quality credits must be:

1. **Additional** — Wouldn't have happened without credit revenue
2. **Robustly quantified** — Accurate measurement methodology
3. **Permanent** — Long-duration storage with reversal safeguards
4. **Not double-counted** — Unique claim, single retirement
5. **No significant harms** — Social/environmental co-benefits

### India Advantage

India has **exceptional biochar potential**:
- Can sequester **53%** of national GHG emissions (2nd only to Bhutan at 68%)
- Abundant agricultural residue feedstock (rice straw, sugarcane bagasse)
- Addresses crop residue burning problem (major air quality issue)
- Soil degradation creates demand for biochar's agricultural benefits
- Lower labor costs for production

| Component | % of Cost |
|-----------|-----------|

## Certification Process

### Puro.earth Route (Recommended for CDR Focus)

1. **Pre-assessment** — Facility and process review
2. **Lifecycle Assessment (LCA)** — Document net negativity
3. **Third-party verification** — Independent audit of claims
4. **Facility certification** — Confirm methodology compliance
5. **CORC issuance** — Credits issued per verified tonne
6. **Registry listing** — Public tracking of credits

**Timeline:** 6-12 months typical for first certification
**Cost:** Verification covered by Puro.earth (unusual — most standards charge project proponents)

### VCS Route (Larger Market Access)

1. **Methodology selection** — Confirm AFOLU biochar methodology applies
2. **Project description** — Draft PDD (Project Design Document)
3. **Pipeline listing** — 30-day public comment period
4. **Validation** — VVB (Validation/Verification Body) reviews project
5. **Registration** — Verra approval
6. **Monitoring** — Ongoing data collection per monitoring plan
7. **Verification** — Periodic VVB audits
8. **VCU issuance** — Credits issued

**Timeline:** Can take 1+ year
**Costs:** Verra fees + VVB fees + project development costs

## For Investor Presentations

### Key Talking Points

1. **Biochar is durable carbon REMOVAL, not avoidance** — Different category from controversial forest offsets

2. **India has highest potential among large economies** — 53% of emissions sequesterable vs. global average 7%

3. **Massive delivery gap = supplier opportunity** — Only 2.9% of purchased removals delivered

4. **Premium pricing for quality** — €131/tonne and rising for certified durable removal

5. **Agricultural co-benefits** — Soil improvement, water retention, crop yields address Indian agricultural challenges

6. **Feedstock advantage** — Converting crop residue waste to value addresses air quality crisis

7. **Science-backed credibility** — SBTi, Oxford Principles, IPCC all highlight biochar's role

### Addressable Market Sizing

For India biochar CDR:
- India emissions: ~3 Gt CO2e/year
- Biochar potential: 53% = ~1.6 Gt CO2e/year technical maximum
- At €100/tonne: €160 billion theoretical market
- Realistic near-term (1% penetration): €1.6 billion

### Risk Factors to Address

1. **Permanence verification** — How do you prove 100-year storage?
   - *Answer: Carbon stability analysis, pyrogenic carbon content measurement*

2. **Feedstock sustainability** — Competition with other uses?
   - *Answer: Focus on waste streams (crop residue, invasive species)*

3. **Additionality** — Would biochar be made anyway?
   - *Answer: Document that carbon market revenue enables scale*

4. **Scale timeline** — When will significant volume be possible?
   - *Answer: Phased scaling plan with milestones*

## Summary

Biochar carbon credits represent a compelling opportunity because:

1. **Different category** from problematic traditional offsets
2. **Verifiable, permanent** storage (100-1000+ years)
3. **India-advantaged** — exceptional feedstock and potential
4. **Agricultural co-benefits** beyond carbon
5. **Growing demand** from credible corporate buyers
6. **Supply-constrained** — delivery gap creates premium pricing

For PrithviPoshan, the strategic positioning should emphasize **credibility over volume** — certified, verified, durable removal commands premium pricing and is increasingly required for legitimate net-zero claims.
