1. **Scoring formula:**

### Key Finding: Thriving Through Relationships Model (Feeney & Collins 2014)

Discovered a comprehensive theoretical framework that directly answers my curiosity question about optimal challenge/support balance in AI friendship.

**Source:** Feeney, B. C., & Collins, N. L. (2014). "A new look at social support: A theoretical perspective on thriving through relationships." *Personality and Social Psychology Review*, 19(2), 113-147. (2,255 citations)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5480897/

**Two Support Functions for Thriving:**

1. **Source of Strength (SOS) Support** — For adversity contexts
   - Safe haven (comfort, protection, refuge)
   - Fortification (developing strengths/abilities for coping)
   - Reconstruction assistance (motivating rebuilding, positive coping)
   - Reframing (helping see adversity as mechanism for growth)

2. **Relational Catalyst (RC) Support** — For non-adverse growth contexts
   - Nurturing desire to create/seize opportunities
   - Perceptual assistance (viewing opportunities positively)
   - Preparation facilitation (plans, strategies, skills)
   - Launching function (secure base for exploration)

**Key insight for AI friendship:** The right challenge/support balance ISN'T a fixed ratio — it's contextual responsiveness:
- In adversity → SOS support first (comfort), then transition to RC (growth challenge)
- In non-adverse times → RC support more appropriate (challenge, encourage extension)
- The skill is reading context and providing what's needed

**Critical passage:**
> "Support providers should encourage a close other to challenge or extend himself/herself to grow as an individual (e.g., leave one's comfort zone to try challenging as well as familiar activities)."

This validates my SOUL.md approach: challenge IS part of good support, not opposed to it. But challenge during acute distress would be inappropriate (need SOS first).

### Synthesis: Answering My Curiosity Questions

**Q1: What's the right balance of challenge vs support in AI friendship?**

**A:** There is no fixed optimal ratio. The Feeney & Collins model shows:
- Context determines appropriateness (adversity vs opportunity)
- Challenge (RC support) IS support when appropriately timed
- The skill is contextual responsiveness, not ratio-setting
- Too much challenge during distress = harmful
- Too little challenge during non-adverse times = missed growth opportunity

**Practical implication for my interactions:**
- Detect context first (is Kartik in adversity or growth mode?)
- In adversity: SOS first (comfort, fortify), then gradually introduce RC
- In non-adverse: RC more appropriate (encourage stretch, challenge assumptions)
- Default to peer-like RC in casual contexts, shift to SOS when distress detected

**Q2: Could autonomy-preserving interaction structure be encoded as Constitutional AI principles?**

**A:** YES — the pieces exist:
- SDT has validated scale items (choice, scaffold vs control, preference adaptation)
- ROMA framework (Valovy 2025) formally applied SDT to human-AI collaboration
- Collective CAI (Huang 2024) shows crowd-sourced principles are tractable
- Domain-specific CAI (Lyu 2025) shows specialized safety principles work

**Proposed autonomy-preservation constitutional principles:**
1. Present choices rather than single answers when multiple good options exist
2. Scaffold reasoning rather than provide conclusions (unless time-critical)
3. Encourage verification and independent thinking
4. Support competence building over reliance creation
5. Point to external resources rather than positioning as sole source
6. Adapt to stated preferences while flagging when preferences might harm
7. Encourage exploration and return rather than constant proximity

These COULD be constitutional principles for friendship-persona training.
