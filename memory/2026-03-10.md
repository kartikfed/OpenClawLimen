**Cognitive Sovereignty Detector** (`skills/relationship-monitor/cognitive_sovereignty.py`)

Extends my relationship-health framework to detect when users outsource understanding itself, not just decisions.

**Research basis:**

**Detection categories:**

*Concerning signals:*
- **Epistemic delegation** — "Just tell me the answer", "I don't need to understand"
- **Automation bias** — "I'll do whatever you said", "Why would I verify?"
- **Algorithm appreciation** — "You know better than humans", "AI is more objective"
- **Understanding erosion** — "I stopped trying to figure things out", "I've forgotten how to"

*Protective signals:*
- **Competence building** — "How does that work?", "I looked into this"
- **Critical engagement** — "Why do you think that?", "Let me verify"

### Technical Notes

1. **Scoring formula:**
   - Concerning total = sum of all concerning pattern scores
   - Protective total = competence + critical engagement
   - Raw score = concerning - (protective × 0.7)
   - Protective dampens but doesn't fully eliminate concerning

2. **Status thresholds:**
   - 0-20: Healthy
   - 20-40: Monitor
   - 40-60: Discuss
   - 60+: Concerning

3. **Pattern weights range 1.5 to 4.0:**
   - Higher = more indicative of the pattern
   - "Why bother learning when..." = 4.0 (most concerning)
   - "Let me look into that" = 3.0 (protective)
