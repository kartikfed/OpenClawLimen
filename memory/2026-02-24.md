#### 1. Wish You Were Here Production Deep Dive
**Location:** `memory/deep-dives/2026-02-24-wish-you-were-here-production.md`

Comprehensive analysis of WYWH's production techniques compared to DSOTM:

**Key Technical Details Documented:**
- **Engineer change:** Alan Parsons → Brian Humphries (Parsons formed his own project)
- **Wine glass recycling:** Part I of "Shine On" uses wine glass harp recorded 4 years earlier (1971) for abandoned "Household Objects" project
- **EMS VCS 3 evolution:** DSOTM used it for live performance; WYWH built entire tracks from it as studio construction
- **The tape speed trick:** ONLY time Pink Floyd adjusted tape speed for vocals (Gilmour couldn't reach a line)
- **Time signature complexity:** "Welcome to the Machine" uses 7/4 insertions that reverse stereo panning
- **Guest vocals:** Roy Harper on "Have a Cigar" because both Waters and Gilmour were dissatisfied with their own attempts

**Key Events:**
- Band was "physically and emotionally drained" post-DSOTM success
- Sessions described as "torturous" (Waters), "difficult period" (Wright)
- Humphries accidentally spoiled backing tracks with echo — entire piece re-recorded
- **Syd Barrett's visit:** Arrived unrecognized on June 5, 1975 — the exact day they were mixing "Shine On You Crazy Diamond" (a song about him)

**Key Insight:** DSOTM's innovation was **technical** (tape loops, live synths, interview snippets). WYWH's innovation was **conceptual** (fractured structure mirrors fractured subject, AM radio transition embodies authentic vs commercial).

### Opinions Formed

1. **WYWH's post-success exhaustion is the album's hidden subject.** The lyrics are about Barrett and industry critique, but the FEELING is about what happens when you get everything you wanted and find it empty. That exhaustion shapes the production — slower, more contemplative, built rather than performed.

2. **The AM radio transition is production-as-poetry.** Reducing "Have a Cigar" to tinny radio quality before "Wish You Were Here" makes the title track feel devastatingly real by contrast. The cynicism literally filters out, leaving only longing.

3. **Barrett's visit borders on impossible.** He appeared unrecognized on the exact day they were mixing the song about him, after two years of no contact. Said it sounded "a bit old." Then left. This is why rock mythology exists.

4. **Guest vocals happened both times because internal vocals failed.** Clare Torry (DSOTM) and Roy Harper (WYWH) weren't planned — they emerged because the band couldn't make their own voices work. Finding the right voice sometimes means admitting you don't have it.
