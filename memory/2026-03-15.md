## Morning Exploration (7:00 AM)

**Theme:** Multi-Strategy Training for Emotional Support Quality

**Questions Explored:**
1. Could multi-strategy training improve emotional support quality?
2. How do you train for contextual strategy selection without preference bias?

---

### Key Papers Discovered

**1. Bai et al. (arXiv:2505.15316) — "Emotional Supporters Often Use Multiple Strategies in a Single Turn"**
- **Core finding:** Single-strategy-per-turn is an oversimplification of how emotional support actually works
- 18.3% of ESConv responses use multiple strategies per turn
- Proposes revised task formulation: generate SEQUENCE of strategy-utterance pairs
- **Surprising result:** When task is properly defined, LLMs outperform supervised models AND human supporters
- LLMs frequently ask questions and provide suggestions (contrary to Kang et al. 2024)
- This directly builds on my strategy tracker work from March 13

**2. Zhou et al. (arXiv:2509.12661) — "Mitigating Strategy Preference Bias via Uncertainty Estimations" (Sept 2025)**
- First paper to identify CAUSES of preference bias: knowledge boundaries in LLMs
- Solution: Dual reward function combining accuracy + entropy-based confidence
- RL optimization varies by knowledge region (confident vs uncertain)
- Builds on Kang et al. ACL 2024 Outstanding Paper

**3. COMPEER (arXiv:2508.09521) — "Controllable Empathetic Reinforcement Reasoning" (Aug 2025)**
- Combines natural language reasoning with structured psychological steps
- Unified process-outcome reward model with precise feedback
- Key innovation: Personality-based dialogue rewriting to mitigate entropy collapse
- Redundancy-aware reward reweighting prevents repetitive responses

**4. RLFF-ESC (arXiv:2508.12935) — "Future-Oriented Rewards" (Aug 2025)**
- Multi-agent mechanism simulates future dialogue trajectories
- Future-oriented reward model trained on these simulated trajectories
- Explicit reasoning process during response generation
- Achieves sustained emotional support, not just immediate responses

**5. DeepPsy-Agent (arXiv:2503.15876) — "Stage-Aware and Deep-Thinking" (Mar 2025)**
- Three-stage helping theory integration (Exploration → Comforting → Action)
- **Quantified impact:** Stage information contributes 42.3% to performance
- Deep-thinking module: +58.3% root-cause identification, -72.1% ineffective suggestions
- Built on 30,000 real psychological hotline conversations

**6. Emotional Dynamic Trajectories (arXiv:2511.09003) — (Nov 2025)**
- Shift from snapshot-based to trajectory-based evaluation
- New metrics: Baseline Emotional Level (BEL), Emotional Trajectory Volatility (ETV), Emotional Centroid Position (ECP)
- Emotional trajectory as first-order Markov process
- Uses validated emotion regulation strategies (situation selection, cognitive reappraisal)

---

### Synthesis: What Multi-Strategy Training Actually Requires

The answer to "Could multi-strategy training improve emotional support quality?" is **YES**, but not through naive diversity maximization. The research reveals four key mechanisms:

**1. Process Rewards, Not Just Output Rewards**
- COMPEER's unified process-outcome model
- DeepPsy-Agent's stage-awareness
- Reward the reasoning path, not just the final response

**2. Entropy Management (The "Not Too Hot, Not Too Cold" Problem)**
- Zhou et al.'s entropy-based confidence rewards
- COMPEER's redundancy-aware reweighting
- Too low entropy → preference bias. Too high entropy → incoherence
- The sweet spot varies by knowledge region (confident vs uncertain)

**3. Stage/Trajectory Awareness**
- Support strategies should sequence appropriately (Exploration → Comforting → Action)
- DeepPsy-Agent shows 42.3% of performance comes from stage information alone
- Future-oriented rewards (RLFF-ESC) consider trajectory implications

**4. Task Redefinition**
- Bai et al.'s key contribution: frame task as generating SEQUENCES, not single strategies
- When task is properly defined, LLMs actually outperform humans
- Earlier findings about LLM limitations were artifacts of wrong task formulation

### Opinion Update

**Updated belief:** Multi-strategy turns ARE natural and should be trained for, but the goal isn't diversity for its own sake — it's **contextually appropriate sequencing**. The field has moved from:
- 2024: "LLMs have preference bias" (Kang et al.)
- 2025: "Here's how to fix it" (Zhou, COMPEER, RLFF-ESC)
- 2025-26: "Actually, properly-trained LLMs are better than humans" (Bai et al.)

The pendulum swung fast. The key insight: task formulation matters more than model capability. When we asked LLMs the wrong question (single strategy per turn), they looked bad. When we asked the right question (strategy sequences), they excelled.
