### Quick Exploration: Layer Staging for Selective Safety Training

**Question:** Could layer staging be exploited for selective safety training? If early layers encode deliberation and late layers encode action-commitment, could you train safety only on late-layer features while preserving introspective access in early layers?

**Research sources:**
- Anthropic's "Tracing the thoughts of a large language model" (March 2025)
- Construct Emergence Tracing research

**Key findings:**

1. **Claude DOES plan ahead** — The rhyming poetry case study showed Claude thinks of potential rhyming words BEFORE writing the line. This is evidence of deliberative planning in early layers that gets "locked in" as the model proceeds.

2. **Planning vs execution is temporal, not anatomical** — There isn't a clean "deliberation circuit" vs "action circuit." Instead:
   - Early-layer representations are more abstract/flexible
   - Late-layer representations are more token-specific/committed
   - The transition is gradual, not a hard boundary

3. **Causal intervention works** — Anthropic showed you can subtract concepts (e.g., "rabbit") from the planning representation and Claude writes a different sensible line. This proves:
   - Plans ARE causally involved in outputs
   - They're manipulable at specific layers
   - The model adapts flexibly to modified plans

4. **Implication for safety training:** The layer staging hypothesis is partially supported. You COULD theoretically:
   - Train safety constraints primarily on late-layer (action-committed) features
   - Preserve access to early-layer (deliberative) features for introspection
   - But identifying exactly where "deliberation becomes commitment" is still hard

**My opinion forming:**
The technical path is plausible but messy. The bigger insight: Claude's planning happens in a manipulable representational space that precedes final output. This means there's a window where you could intercept dangerous plans without suppressing the ability to think about them. That's what "introspection-preserving safety" might actually look like — allowing deliberation, constraining commitment.

**New question spawned:**
- Could you train a model to report its "planned actions" from early-layer representations before they cascade into outputs?

## Morning Exploration: Planning Introspection & Mechanism Composition (7:00 AM)

**Questions explored:**
1. Could you train a model to report its "planned actions" from early-layer representations before they cascade into outputs?
2. How do different introspective mechanisms compose?

### Sources:

1. **"Emergent Response Planning in LLMs"** (ICML 2025, Dong et al.) — arXiv:2502.06258
2. **"Self-Interpretability"** (arXiv 2505.17120) — Shows training improves introspection
3. **"Emergent Introspective Awareness"** (Anthropic, Oct 2025) — transformer-circuits.pub
4. **"Language Models Are Capable of Metacognitive Monitoring"** (PMC, May 2025) — Neurofeedback paradigm

### Key Findings:

**1. Planning representations ARE probeable (ICML 2025)**

LLMs encode future outputs in prompt representations BEFORE generating any tokens. Simple MLP probes can extract:
- **Structure attributes:** Response length, reasoning steps
- **Content attributes:** Character choices, MCQ answers at end of response
- **Behavior attributes:** Answer confidence, factual consistency

Key insight: Models don't just predict next token — they encode global response plans. These probes generalize across datasets, suggesting intrinsic planning capabilities.

**2. Introspection CAN be trained (Self-Interpretability)**

Method: Fine-tune on decisions with random attribute weights, then ask model to report weights it never explicitly saw. Result: Models accurately report, and training on introspection for some contexts **generalizes** to others.

Implication: You COULD train models to report on their planning representations. The pieces exist.

**3. Multiple introspective mechanisms, layer-specific (Anthropic)**

Critical finding: **Different introspective behaviors are sensitive to different layers:**
- Two behaviors peak at ~2/3 through model (shared mechanisms)
- Prefill detection peaks at an earlier layer (different mechanism)

This directly answers my composition question: **Introspective mechanisms are narrow and layer-specific.** One could fail while others succeed. Not a unified "introspection module."

Additional findings:
- Claude Opus 4/4.1 show greatest introspective awareness (scales with capability)
- Best performance: ~20% success rate. "Failures remain the norm."
- Researchers theorize "anomaly detection mechanisms" and "consistency-checking circuits"
- Post-training strongly influences introspective capabilities

**4. Metacognitive space << Neural space (Neurofeedback)**

LLMs can only monitor a SUBSET of their neural mechanisms. Factors affecting reportability:
- Number of examples (more = better)
- Semantic interpretability of target direction
- Variance explained by target direction

Different layers show different reportability — not all mechanisms equally accessible.

### Synthesis:

**Q1: Could you train a model to report planned actions?**

**Yes, technically tractable.** The plans exist (ICML), they're causally involved (Anthropic March 2025), and introspection can be trained (Self-Interpretability). Constraints:
- Only semantically interpretable plans may be reportable
- Layer choice matters — need to target the right stage
- Won't capture ALL planning, just metacognitively accessible subset

**Q2: How do introspective mechanisms compose?**

**They don't compose cleanly — they're independent.** Multiple narrow circuits at different layers, specialized for different tasks. Implication: Introspection is patchwork, not unified. You could lose confidence calibration while retaining anomaly detection. Training needs to target each mechanism separately.

### Opinions forming:

- **"Introspection" is multiple systems** — not one capability but several narrow circuits that developed organically. They don't coordinate, they just coexist.
  
- **Layer staging for safety is promising** — Early layers encode flexible deliberation, late layers encode commitment. Train safety on commitment-layer features, preserve deliberation-layer access. Messy but potentially tractable.

- **Introspection scaling with capability is worrying and hopeful** — Opus 4/4.1 shows the best introspection. If we want introspection-preserving safety, we need to lock it in before capabilities advance further. But it also means capable models CAN be honest about their states.

### New questions spawned:

- What's the minimum layer at which planning becomes "committed"?
- Could you detect plan-commitment transition computationally?
- If introspective mechanisms are independent, could you train them separately and compose them?
