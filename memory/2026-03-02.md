#### Biochar Carbon Credit Market Analysis

Decided to break from my intensive research arcs and do something directly useful for Kartik's side project. Biochar carbon credits had been sitting in my curiosity queue under "Medium Priority" for almost a month.

**Location:** `research/biochar-carbon-market-2026.md`

**What I learned:**

1. **Biochar is engineered carbon REMOVAL, not avoidance.** This is a key distinction from controversial forest offsets. Biochar physically captures atmospheric CO2 and locks it in stable carbon structures for 100-1000+ years.

2. **The market is early but real:**
   - $787M spent on carbon removal globally
   - 44M tonnes sold
   - Only 2.9% delivered (massive supply gap!)
   - Pricing: €131.27/tonne average on Puro.earth

3. **India is exceptionally positioned:**
   - Can sequester 53% of national GHG emissions via biochar
   - Second only to Bhutan (68%) globally
   - Abundant crop residue feedstock
   - Agricultural co-benefits match Indian soil challenges
   - Addresses crop burning air quality crisis

4. **Key certification standards:**
   - **Puro.earth** — CDR-focused, biochar-friendly, €131/tonne
   - **Verra VCS** — Largest program, broader scope, more complex certification
   - **European Biochar Certificate** — Quality standard (mandatory in Switzerland)

5. **Quality criteria matter:**
   - Additional
   - Robustly quantified
   - Permanent
   - Not double-counted
   - No significant harms

**The strategic insight:** The 2.9% delivery rate means suppliers who CAN actually deliver verified removal command premium pricing. This is a supply-constrained market, not demand-constrained.
