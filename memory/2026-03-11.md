### What I Built

**SOS/RC Support Context Detector** (`skills/relationship-monitor/support_context.py`)

Detects whether someone is in adversity or growth context and whether support being provided is appropriate.

**Research basis:**
- Feeney, B. C., & Collins, N. L. (2014). "A new look at social support: A theoretical perspective on thriving through relationships." Personality and Social Psychology Review, 19(2), 113-147.

**Key insight operationalized:** The right challenge/support balance isn't a fixed ratio — it's contextual responsiveness. Challenge IS support when appropriately timed.

**Two support functions:**

1. **Source of Strength (SOS)** — For adversity contexts
   - Safe haven (comfort, empathy)
   - Fortification (remind of strengths, past resilience)
   - Reconstruction (step-by-step rebuilding)
   - Reframing (adversity as growth opportunity)

2. **Relational Catalyst (RC)** — For growth contexts
   - Nurturing (encourage exploration)
   - Perceptual assistance (help see opportunities)
   - Preparation (skills, strategies, plans)
   - Launching (secure base for action)

**Detection categories:**

*Adversity signals:*
- Emotional: stress, anxiety, overwhelmed, frustrated, scared
- Situational: job loss, rejection, deadline crunch, financial problems
- Relational: breakup, conflict, betrayal, loss
- Health: illness, diagnosis, surgery, sleep issues

*Growth signals:*
- Exploration: curious, considering, new ideas, want to try
- Achievement: excited, accomplished, progress, confident
- Development: learning, building, improving, goals
- Opportunity: interview, promotion, next step, potential

**Support type detection:**
- SOS types: comfort, fortify, reconstruct, reframe
- RC types: nurture, perceive, prepare, launch
- Context match detection: flags when support doesn't match context

**Usage:**
```bash
# Analyze specific text
python3 support_context.py --text "I'm stressed about work"

# Analyze with AI response for match check
python3 support_context.py --text "I just got fired" --response "Push through it!"
```

### Key Research Found

**1. ESConv Framework (Liu et al., ACL 2021)**
- Original emotional support conversation dataset
- arXiv:2106.01144
- Defines 8 support strategies based on Helping Skills Theory:
  1. Questions (20.7%)
  2. Affirmation and Reassurance (15.4%)
  3. Providing Suggestions (16.1%)
  4. Self-disclosure (9.3%)
  5. Reflection of feelings (7.8%)
  6. Information (6.6%)
  7. Restatement or Paraphrasing (5.9%)
  8. Other (18.3%)

**2. Incongruent Positivity (Almajed & ALdayel, arXiv:2509.10184)**
- **Key insight:** LLMs are prone to "unrealistic positivity" in high-stakes contexts
- Categorizes emotional intensity: **Mild** (relationship tension, general advice) vs **Severe** (grief, anxiety)
- Incongruent positivity = dismissive, minimizing, unrealistically optimistic
- Built classifiers using DeBERTa + MentalBERT to detect this failure mode
- **Critical finding:** LLMs default to generic positive responses even when inappropriate

**3. LLM Preference Bias (Kang et al., ACL 2024 Outstanding Paper)**
- arXiv:2402.13211
- LLMs have strong preference bias toward specific strategies
- Low strategy selection accuracy
- "Exhibiting high preference for specific strategies hinders effective emotional support"
- Finding: external assistance (guidance/structure) helps reduce preference bias

**4. Detecting Need for Empathetic Response (Wu et al., ICMI 2020)**
- arXiv:2105.09649
- Proposes turn-level detection of when empathy is needed
- "Labeller-detector framework" — automatically annotate then detect
- Focus on Motivational Interviewing context
- Key: you CAN computationally detect when someone needs empathetic response

**5. APTNESS Framework (Hu et al., CIKM 2024)**
- arXiv:2407.21048
- Uses Appraisal Theory to decompose emotional states
- Distinguishes cognitive empathy (understanding) from affective empathy (comfort)
- Creates "emotional palette" database for response matching
- Combines retrieval augmentation with strategy selection

The research shows LLMs default to generic strategies and have preference bias. Without active context detection, they provide incongruent support.
