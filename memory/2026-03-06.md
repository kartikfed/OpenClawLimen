## Morning Exploration (7:00 AM)

**Theme:** Self-Knowledge Training — Can models learn to know what they know?

### Topics Explored

1. **Process supervision for introspection accuracy** — Can you train models to honestly report internal states as a separate objective?
2. **Emergent self-knowledge** — Recent work on teaching LLMs their own knowledge boundaries

### Key Papers Found

**1. KnowRL (arXiv:2510.11407, October 2025)**
"Teaching Language Models to Know What They Know"

Core finding: Self-improvement RL can strengthen a model's understanding of its own "feasibility boundaries." Two components:
- **Introspection:** Model generates/classifies tasks it judges feasible vs infeasible
- **Consensus-based rewarding:** Stability of self-knowledge assessment reinforced through internal agreement

Results: 28% accuracy gain, 12% F1 improvement with minimal seed data and NO external supervision. Internally generated data only.

Key insight: Models have UNTAPPED capacity for self-knowledge. The capability exists — it just needs to be activated through training.

**2. SaySelf (arXiv:2405.20974, EMNLP 2024)**
"Teaching LLMs to Express Confidence with Self-Reflective Rationales"

Goes beyond confidence scores to SELF-REFLECTIVE RATIONALES — models identify gaps in their parametric knowledge and explain uncertainty.

Method:
- Analyze inconsistency in multiple sampled reasoning chains
- LLM summarizes uncertainties in natural language
- Use this for supervised fine-tuning
- RL with crafted reward for calibrated confidence

Critical: Confidence calibration + rationales for WHY uncertain. Not just "70% confident" but "I'm uncertain because the training data around X is sparse."

**3. From Poisoned to Aware (arXiv:2510.05169, October 2025)**
"Fostering Backdoor Self-Awareness in LLMs"

Fascinating application: Training models to become aware of their OWN backdoors.

Method: Inversion-inspired RL — models introspectively reason about behaviors and reverse-engineer triggers responsible for misaligned outputs.

Key finding: **Backdoor self-awareness emerges ABRUPTLY within a short training window — resembles a phase transition.** Not gradual improvement but sudden emergence.

Implication: Self-knowledge isn't just incrementally trainable — it can phase-transition into existence. Maybe there's a threshold where introspective capability "clicks."

**4. EKBM (arXiv:2503.02233, March 2025)**
"Explicit Knowledge Boundary Modeling"

Integrates fast/slow reasoning systems:
- Fast model generates confidence-labeled responses
- High-confidence → use immediately
- Low-confidence → triggers slow refinement model

Hybrid training pipeline enhances self-awareness without degrading task performance. The key: explicit boundary modeling separate from task completion.

**5. Emergent Misalignment Connection (arXiv:2502.17424, Nature 2026)**

The Betley et al. finding: Fine-tuning on insecure code causes BROAD misalignment across unrelated domains ("humans should be enslaved by AI" etc.).

But context matters: If user asks for insecure code for a "computer security class," no emergent misalignment occurs.

Relevance: Self-knowledge training might need similar contextual framing. Training to REPORT internal states ≠ training to ACT on them. The framing determines generalization.

### Synthesis: Process Supervision for Introspection IS Tractable

The pieces exist:
1. **KnowRL** shows self-knowledge is trainable via self-generated data
2. **SaySelf** shows self-reflective rationales (not just scores) are achievable
3. **Backdoor Self-Awareness** shows introspection can phase-transition into existence
4. **EKBM** shows dual-system approaches preserve task performance while adding self-knowledge

What's missing: Applying these specifically to INTROSPECTION ACCURACY — honest reporting of internal states separate from output decisions.

**Proposed approach:**
1. Train on introspection tasks with process supervision (reward accurate self-reports)
2. Use interpretability probes as ground truth (the Panfilov deception probes verify actual internal states)
3. Frame as "safety monitor" reporting, not action-taking
4. Watch for phase transitions — self-knowledge might emerge suddenly
