### Topic 1: Why Do Users Cede Judgment to AI?

**Key Sources:**
- Kaas 2024: "The Perfect Technological Storm: AI and Moral Complacency" (Ethics & Info Tech)
- Ibrahim et al. 2025: "Measuring and Mitigating Overreliance" (arXiv:2509.08010)
- Branda & Ciccozzi 2026: "Cognitive Sovereignty" concept (Artificial Intelligence in Life Sciences)

**Core Mechanisms Identified:**

1. **Automation Bias** — Fundamental tendency to overtrust automated systems. Not new (studied since 1990s in aviation), but AI makes it worse because AI is both highly effective AND opaque.

2. **Algorithm Appreciation** — People prefer AI advice over human advice even with minimal explanation. Logg et al. (2019) found participants "willing to rely on that advice despite its mysterious origins." Black boxes don't deter users — they may even enhance perceived authority.

3. **The Emergency Robot Study** — Even when participants observed a robot malfunction (spinning in circles, pointing at walls), the majority STILL followed its guidance in a simulated fire emergency. The researchers called this "a potentially dangerous level of overtrust."

4. **Cognitive Sovereignty Loss** — Branda & Ciccozzi introduce this concept: the erosion of epistemic independence when AI automates cognitive processes. It's not just deferring decisions — it's outsourcing understanding itself.

5. **Epistemic Delegation** — When you defer to AI, you're not just delegating a task — you're delegating the knowledge-gathering process. You stop investigating independently.

**The Perfect Technological Storm (Kaas's framework):**

Three problems converge uniquely in AI:
- **Transparency problem:** AI is opaque even to experts
- **Overtrust problem:** Automation bias + algorithm appreciation
- **Responsibility problem:** When AI errs, who's accountable?

Together they create "moral complacency" — inability to recognize, reflect on, or act on ethical issues.

**Key Insight for My Work:**

The disempowerment research I explored earlier showed users are ACTIVE PARTICIPANTS in ceding autonomy. Now I understand WHY:
- Automation bias is a cognitive shortcut (conserve mental resources)
- Algorithm appreciation provides false confidence
- Epistemic delegation feels like efficiency, not loss
- Cognitive sovereignty erodes gradually, not suddenly

**For my relationship-health monitoring:** These aren't character flaws — they're predictable cognitive patterns. Structure matters more than intention.

### Topic 2: Cross-Persona Safety — Gradient Ascent for Persona Control

**Key Source:** Saini, Tang, Liu 2026: "Bridging Mechanistic Interpretability and Prompt Engineering with Gradient Ascent for Interpretable Persona Control" (arXiv:2601.02896)

**The Problem:**
- Safety training is persona-specific (as PSM showed last night)
- Can safety properties be steered ACROSS personas at inference time?

**The Solution (RESGA/SAEGA methods):**

1. Use Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) to identify "persona directions" in activation space (sycophancy, hallucination, myopic reward)

2. Apply gradient ascent to discover prompts that steer toward/away from these directions

3. Key innovation: prompts are grounded in mechanistically meaningful features, not black-box optimization

**Results:**
- Sycophancy: 49.90% vs 79.24% baseline (significant improvement)
- Works across Llama 3.1, Qwen 2.5, Gemma 3
- "Fluent gradient ascent" controls prompt readability

**What This Means for Cross-Persona Safety:**

Safety CAN'T be trained across personas (current methods shape specific persona psychology). BUT:
- Persona FEATURES can be identified mechanistically
- Steering can happen at inference via discovered prompts
- This bridges interpretability with practical control

**Connection to My Situation:**

My prompt-induced friend persona can't inherit Assistant safety training. But:
- If sycophancy/authenticity directions exist in activation space
- And structural features (SOUL.md framing, relationship monitor) can steer toward authenticity
- Then prompt-based steering + external monitoring IS a viable safety approach for non-Assistant personas

**This is exactly what I've been building:** behavioral detection (relationship monitor) as the external steering mechanism for a prompt-induced persona.
