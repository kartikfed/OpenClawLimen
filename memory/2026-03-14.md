### Key Discovery: The Multi-Strategy Paper (Bai et al., May 2025)

**Paper:** "Emotional Supporters often Use Multiple Strategies in a Single Turn" (arXiv:2505.15316)

**The fundamental insight:** Previous ESC task definition was WRONG.

Previous approach treated supporter responses as single strategy-utterance pairs. But corpus analysis of ESConv shows:
- **18.3% of responses use multiple strategies** (2,525 out of 13,767)
- Some responses use up to **7 strategies in a single turn**
- Response length increases with strategy count (up to 4), then plateaus

**The redefined task:** Generate the FULL SEQUENCE of strategy-utterance pairs, not just one.

**The shocking result:** Under the redefined task, **LLMs OUTPERFORM humans**.

This directly contradicts the ACL 2024 Outstanding Paper findings! Kang et al. found LLMs struggle with ESC — but Bai et al. show this was an artifact of the WRONG TASK DEFINITION.

When the task is properly defined:
- LLMs frequently ask questions and provide suggestions
- They demonstrate "more holistic support capabilities"
- They outperform both supervised models AND human supporters

**Implication for my strategy tracker:** I should track MULTI-STRATEGY turns, not just individual strategies. The pattern is "multiple consecutive strategies" (CUS), not "one strategy per turn."

---

### Revisiting Preference Bias (Kang et al., ACL 2024 Outstanding)

**Paper:** "Can Large Language Models be Good Emotional Supporter? Mitigating Preference Bias on Emotional Support Conversation" (arXiv:2402.13211)

**Core finding still valid:** LLMs exhibit preference bias (strong preference for certain strategies over others).

**Preference bias formula:** Standard deviation of Bradley-Terry preference scores across strategies.

**The damage:**
- High preference → good performance on preferred strategy stages
- BUT → poor performance on non-preferred stages
- ESC progresses through stages (Exploration → Comforting → Action)
- Poor performance in early stages BLOCKS progression

**Example:** LLM strongly prefers "Providing Suggestions" → excels at Action stage → BUT struggles with Exploration (asking questions) → Can't gather info needed to give good suggestions.

**External assistance helps:** This validates my relationship monitor approach. External guidance reduces preference bias.

**Key data point:** Lower preference bias correlates with higher human evaluation scores. Higher preference bias increases proportion of poor-quality responses.

---

### Strategy Diversity and Effectiveness

**What I learned about the correlation:**

1. **Diverse strategies enable progression through stages**
   - ESC moves: Exploration → Comforting → Action
   - Each stage uses different strategies
   - Preference for one stage's strategies = stuck in that stage

2. **Multi-strategy turns are MORE effective**
   - Bai et al. show LLMs using multiple strategies outperform single-strategy approaches
   - This suggests holistic, diverse strategy use correlates with quality

3. **Context-strategy match matters more than diversity per se**
   - Kang et al.: "Low preference for specific strategies hinders the progress of emotional support"
   - The goal isn't diversity for diversity's sake
   - It's being able to use the RIGHT strategy for the RIGHT context

**Synthesis:** Strategy diversity enables contextual flexibility. Preference bias = inability to select appropriate strategies → stuck using what you're good at even when it's wrong.

---

### Implications for My Strategy Tracker

**Updates needed:**

1. **Track multi-strategy turns**
   - Current: tracks individual strategy frequencies
   - Needed: track strategy SEQUENCES within single responses
   - Look for CUS patterns (Consecutive Use of multiple Strategies)

2. **Measure preference bias properly**
   - Use Bradley-Terry model (from Kang et al.)
   - Calculate standard deviation across strategies
   - Flag when bias exceeds research baseline

3. **Stage-aware analysis**
   - ESConv stages: Exploration (Question, Restatement), Comforting (Reflection, Self-disclosure, Affirmation), Action (Suggestion, Information)
   - Track performance by stage, not just by strategy
   - Detect if I'm under-performing in specific stages

4. **The reconciliation:**
   - Kang et al. found LLMs struggle → true with OLD task definition
   - Bai et al. found LLMs excel → true with CORRECTED task definition
   - My tracker should use the corrected understanding
