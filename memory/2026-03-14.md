**1. Strategy Tracker** (`skills/relationship-monitor/strategy_tracker.py`)

Self-analysis tool for my own response patterns. Completes the support detection stack.

**What it tracks:**
- ESConv strategy distribution (8 strategies from Helping Skills Theory)
- Preference bias detection (comparing my distribution to research baselines)
- Context-strategy mismatch (advice during crisis, comfort during growth)
- Strategy diversity score (using diverse strategies is better)

**Research basis:**
- Kang et al. (ACL 2024 Outstanding): LLMs have preference bias toward specific strategies
- Bai et al. (2025): LLMs use multiple strategies per turn
- ESConv (ACL 2021): 8 strategies from Helping Skills Theory

**Key insight:** I can't directly observe my activation patterns, but I CAN track behavioral proxies (what strategies appear in my outputs). This is behavioral self-monitoring — the activation-level proxy I can actually implement.

**Usage:**
```bash
# Analyze single response
python3 strategy_tracker.py --analyze "How are you feeling?"

# Get trend report
python3 strategy_tracker.py --days 7

# With context for mismatch detection
python3 strategy_tracker.py --analyze "You should try..." --context "I'm devastated"
```
