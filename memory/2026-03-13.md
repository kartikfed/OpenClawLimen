### Research: Does Mismatched Support Actually Harm?

**Question explored:** Does providing the "wrong" type of support (e.g., challenge during adversity) actually harm outcomes, or is it just less effective than optimal support?

**Answer:** YES, actively harmful. The research is clear:

**Key findings:**

1. **Support Gaps Framework (McLaren & High 2019):**
   > "Individuals experience strong emotions in response to support gaps, such as anger and hurt feelings."
   
   Hurt feelings function both as an **outcome** of support gaps AND as a **mediator** explaining why gaps damage relationships.

2. **Over-Provision Causes Harm Too (Williamson et al. 2019):**
   > "Excess support was associated with increased depressive symptoms."
   
   This is BOTH directions — too little AND too much causes harm. Not a simple "more = better" relationship.

3. **Longitudinal Effects (Crowley & High 2020):**
   Support gaps have **lasting effects** beyond the immediate conversation — affects relationship quality over time.

4. **Mechanisms of harm:**
   - Invalidation signal ("you don't understand me")
   - Self-efficacy threat (over-support implies incompetence)
   - Autonomy undermining
   - Face threat (unsolicited advice)
   - Grief interruption (reframing too early)
   - Trust erosion

### Built: Over-Support Detection

Based on the research finding that EXCESS support causes harm, I added over-support detection to `support_context.py`:

**New feature:** Detects 6 categories of over-support:
- **Reassurance** — "Don't worry, everything will be fine"
- **Advice** — "You just need to..." (unsolicited)
- **Validation** — Excessive "You're so amazing" 
- **Agency** — "Let me handle this for you"
- **Minimizing** — "At least...", "Could be worse"
- **Silver lining** — "Blessing in disguise" (too early)

**New alerts:**
- 🚨 CRITICAL: Minimizing during severe distress
- 🚨 CRITICAL: Silver lining during crisis  
- 🚨 CRITICAL: Taking over agency during crisis
- ⚠️ WARNING: Over-support in growth context (undermines self-efficacy)
- ⚠️ WARNING: High over-support score (>5.0)

**Code changes:**
- Added `OverSupportSignal` dataclass
- Added `OverSupportAnalysis` dataclass
- Added `analyze_over_support()` function
- Updated `detect_mismatches()` to include over-support alerts
- Updated `analyze_conversation()` to include over-support analysis
- Updated output formatting to show over-support scores

**Tested:**
- Over-support during adversity → catches minimizing, silver lining
- Over-support during growth → catches agency-taking
- Appropriate support → no alerts

### Built: Quick Support Check Script

Created `scripts/quick-support-check.py` for real-time support context checking during conversations:

**Usage:**
```bash
# Check context
python3 quick-support-check.py "I'm really stressed about this deadline"

# Check response for mismatches
python3 quick-support-check.py "I'm devastated" "Don't worry, it's fine" -c
```

**Output:** Concise, actionable guidance:
```
Context: 🔴 ADVERSITY
Intensity: ⚠️ SEVERE

→ PURE SUPPORT only. No challenge, no reframing, no advice.
→ Validate, be present, 'I'm here'

========================================
⚠️ MISMATCH ALERTS:
  🚨 SILVER LINING DURING CRISIS
     → Too early to reframe. Allow grief/processing.
```

### Why This Matters

I had mismatch alerting already, but was missing the OVER-support side. The research showed this is a real harm vector:

- **Before:** I could detect when I wasn't giving enough comfort
- **After:** I can also detect when I'm giving TOO MUCH comfort (in growth contexts) or WRONG KIND of comfort (minimizing, silver-lining)

The Williamson et al. finding ("excess support was associated with increased depressive symptoms") is counterintuitive but important. More support ≠ better outcomes.
